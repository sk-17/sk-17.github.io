BCG Onwards & Upwards

Visualizing the natural imperative of growth🏭🌿

After the 2016 TED@BCG conference, BCG wanted to emphasize its perspective on business growth. Working closely with design director Steve Haslip at Carbone Smolan Agency, I designed and animated a video ad that would air on TED.com.

Noticing a connection in BCG’s writing between the evolution of natural systems and business growth, I developed a logic of motion that blends the organic with the industrial.

Background

TED@BCG is an ongoing collaboration between BCG and TED.com featuring a diverse array of speakers from the BCG community. CSA designs the annual conference identity, promotional materials, and a microsite to curate the talks and related content.

After the conference, a promotional campaign runs on TED.com, including post-roll video ads. As part of this larger design system, the video had to be visually similar.

Defining a visual language

The existing artwork consisted of only a few collages of prismatic shapes. Steve and I worked together to expand just a few static images into a system that could hold attention for over a minute.

We engaged the team at BCG in curating the visual direction. Presenting BCG with a variety of styleframes, they narrowed the options down to a handful of frames which later informed our stylistic decisions.

Growth is flow

BCG often related the practice of growing a business to growth systems found in nature. For both nature and business, growth is an omnipresent necessity. From this insight, I developed a logic of motion that blends together organic, flowing motion, with more rigid, mechanical lockups.

Seeing green?

While developing storyboards, we notice an overwhelming presence of green. BCG’s signature color couldn’t be removed entirely, but the video was at risk of feeling too static.

Steve suggested we use color to add a sense of progression. From scene to scene, different colors leak into the frame and blend together, adding dynamism without distracting from the text on screen.

The Outcome

People are more likely to be listening to a TED talk than watching it. We initially suggested using voice-over, but BCG requested that all text be presented visually. We had to scale back the motion to keep the text readable.

In an ideal world, a script with a stronger story arc and clearer message, delivered as voice over, would have created a more impactful video.
