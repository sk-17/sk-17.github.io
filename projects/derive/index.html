Derive

Enabling creative discovery on the web💭

Search engines inhibit discovery because they require known keywords. Could the way we search for information be restructured to encourage creative discovery?

Over the course of 3 weeks, I conceptualized a new platform for searching the web and discovering new ideas. Derive structures the web search as a journey from one idea to another.

Background

We move through life bumping into one idea after the next, building new connections and formulating new ideas. The Internet should be the perfect space for this brushing up of information to occur, but with so much content, how do you filter out the noise?

Search engines fail because they rely on the user already having a keyword in mind. I set out to envision a new content discovery platform that encourages serendipitous discoveries while still allowing for targeted research.

The Search as a journey

Taking a walk is one of the best ways to expose oneself to new ideas and discover creative moments. Wandering through the city streets encourages serendipitous discovery, but you still have control over where you’re going and know how to navigate the space.

Looking at web searches as one-way journeys allowed me to establish a clear structure to the flow of information.

Information Architecture

Because I was reinventing how we search the web, I first developed a clear framework for presenting different topics and search results.

Since Derive displays a mix of images, videos and text at varying sizes, presenting search results on cards gave the information a consistent structure.

While conducting usability tests through several iterations, participants unanimously agreed that swiping through search results was the most intuitive way of navigating the content.

Discovering new ideas

At the core of the app’s functionality is the Derive button, which allows users to derive a new topic from their current collection of ideas.

In my initial prototypes, the button was buried within other actions, making its functionality unclear. For the final iteration, I cut down on visual noise as much as possible and emphasized the app’s primary functions.

Some things stored

What’s the use of discovering new ideas if you can’t revisit them later? When you finish a journey, any cards you saved are collected in the Past Journeys area.

If a search is initiated and no ideas are saved, the collection isn’t recorded in Past Journeys.

The outcome

Tackling such a conceptual problem in just a few weeks is basically setting yourself up for failure.

Even though I focused on developing the most essential functions, the interactions could be a bit more intuitive. If I had more time I would have explored motion further to help users orient themselves in the app.
