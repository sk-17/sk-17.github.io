Happening

Delivering a personalized experience through conversation📰

Conversational UI has been touted as the future of user interface design. Whether or not this is viable (probably not), the one thing most messaging platforms lack is effective presentation of embedded content.

To improve the experience of consuming rich content in conversation, I conceptualized a news app over the course of three weeks. The result mixes the intimacy of messaging with the content accessibility of traditional news apps.

Setting Goals

To focus my research, I conducted a brief competitor audit of news and messaging apps and interviewed my target audience to get a sense of what features to prioritize. From the interviews, I constructed several user personas against which I could test my decisions.

I honed in on the following key objectives:
  1. develop an effective method of displaying content within messaging flows
  2. explore conversation as a platform for delivering personalized content
  3. leverage natural language processing to perform complex actions (like setting custom news updates)
Delivering a personal experience

Natural language processing has the capacity to provide a highly personalized experience. All interactions in Happening occur through conversation with the AI presence, Roger.

Roger can engage in conversations about breaking news and deliver bespoke briefings on almost any topic upon request.

By shifting the focus to conversation, requesting daily news updates becomes a simple question instead of endless responses to a complicated form-based interface.

Contextual shortcuts

To reduce the amount of typing necessary, a row of shortcut buttons provides easy access to frequently-used features. The buttons change contextually to show actions that are relevant to the current conversation.

Collapsing flow

The main goal of this exploration was to find a better way to display embedded messaging content. I decided to present each news story on a card with an emphasized headlines and image to allow for quick scanning.

During user testing, a concern was that the feed took up a lot of screen real estate and caused the messages to get buried.

To resolve this, the feed collapses into a horizontally scrolling list when new messages appear at the end of the conversation.

the outcome

Happening was conceived before the current political situation developed. There is now a greater need than ever to combat misinformation and the bubble effect of social media, and I regret not addressing it with this project.
