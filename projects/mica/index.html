MICA Holiday Card

Helping a leading art college say Happy New Year🎉

MICA wanted a video holiday card to email to donors, alumni and peer institutions. With a new course structure and new design center under construction, the school was repositioning itself to prepare artists for the 21st century.

With a turnaround time of just two weeks, I put together a video that positions MICA as a hub of innovation and social change and showcases the school’s new visual identity.

Finding words

To kick off the project, I met with Debra Rubino, MICA’s VP of strategic communications to set a schedule and discuss the video's message.

The card needed to celebrate MICA as a place of tremendous creativity, innovation and social change. I had to find a way to tie these three disciplines together and reflect the diversity of MICA's community.

After building some word lists, I noticed an abundance of bold, active words like making, thinking, and shaping. I developed a script that emphasized the significance of creative actions in both artistic practice and social change-making.

New year, new identity

Along with a refreshed curriculum, MICA updated its visual identity to make it feel more contemporary. The new brand hadn’t been rolled out yet, and a holiday card was the perfect way to unveil it.

Knowing that I would be working with text on screen, I avoided MICA's signature patterns and instead focused on bringing to life the new color palette and image treatments.

Providing Options

Despite the limited time frame, I developed an alternate version of the video with a stronger emphasis on the holiday greeting.

Together, Debra and I perfected the message and flow of both concepts before presenting the to MICA’s president.

Option A: all about MICA and the new brand.

Option B: a more direct holiday greeting.

The outcome

In the end, out of options designed by several students, President Hoi selected the brand-focused concept for distribution. Members of administration emailed the card to peer institutions, key influencers, donors and alumni. The Division of Strategic Communications received an overwhelmingly positive response from the video.
